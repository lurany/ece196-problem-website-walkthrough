Problem

Problem Definition

UCSD students who use campus courts such as basketball and badminton do not have a reliable way to know how crowded the courts are before arriving. This can lead to wasted time walking to the courts only to find that there is no space to play, or missing chances to play when the courts are actually open.

Problem Motivation

Our team chose this problem because students often have to gamble whether the courts will be open or crowded. Right now, many students rely on fixed schedules, but those do not provide reliable real time information. We wanted to focus on a common campus problem that affects recreation and convenience.
